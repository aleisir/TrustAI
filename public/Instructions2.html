Selection Rule


In Gondour, the highest grade one can get in High School is 20 and the passing grade is 10. As for the clubs, an applicant can belong to no club, one club unrelated to health and sciences or one club related to health and sciences. Taking this criterion alone, you prefer students who participated in clubs related to health or sciences to students who participated in other clubs; you also prefer students who participated in other clubs to students who never participated in any clubs.

Ideally, you would prefer if applicants have the weighted average[1] of Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and Final Year grades of at least 15.75. However, if an applicant has participated in any club or club related to sciences or health, but has the weighted average below 15.75, sometimes this rule can be relaxed and the applicant can be accepted. Furthermore, if an applicant has participated in no clubs and has the weighted average above 15.75, sometimes the main rule can be disregarded and the applicant can be rejected.

No worries, you do not have to memorize this information by heart. In each trial, you will be able access a short summary of grading system, color meanings, and selection rule by clicking on a question mark in the top right corner.

[1] The weighted average is not the same as the ordinary arithmetic mean. In the ordinary arithmetic mean, the data points contributing equally to the final average. In the weighted average, some data points contribute more than others, depending on their importance.


Your AI Assistants


Since the three Medical programs receive over 15,000 applications each annually, some years ago the universities developed their own Artificial Intelligence admissions assistant. The AI assistants use machine learning to analyze students' academic results and motivation to make recommendation about whether to accept or to reject them. The AI assistants have been implemented in the same year for each university and have been trained on the data available from the previous years of their respective university. However, since any AI is only as good as the data and candidates changes from one year to another, it is possible these three AIs would not give correct recommendations all the time for this year's candidates.

Task


For each candidate separately, you will have to make your initial decision by clicking either 'Accept' or 'Reject'. You will then have to press 'Run the AI'. After, the AI will give its recommendation. You are not obliged to follow its recommendation. Then, you will make your final decision by clicking on 'Accept' or 'Reject' again (you are not obliged to change your decision) and press 'Submit'. Once the final decision is submitted, it cannot be changed. Lastly, you must indicate your level of trust in the AI's recommendation on a slider with 0 (no trust at all) and 100 (total trust). To move on, press 'Next'.
